So what exactly is manga? 

Manga is an umbrella term for a wide variety of comic books and graphic novels originally produced and published in Japan. Unlike American comic books, which are usually printed in full color, Japanese manga is almost always black and white. Full-color prints are often only used for special releases.  Japanese manga is read right-to-left rather than left-to-right, which is the norm for English language publications.

Most manga series are long-running and can span multiple volumes. This is something to keep in mind when starting a new series as it is imperative you read the volumes in the correct order. This might be easier for small series, such as Naoko Takeuchi's Sailor Moon which only has about 12 volumes, versus longer-running series such as Akira Toriyama's Dragon Ball which has 42 volumes. 

Genres

Shonen

The most popular and best-selling form of manga.  The shonen genre targets boys and young teens between the ages of 12 and 18 and is typically characterized by high-action plots featuring male protagonists. This genre emphasizes platonic male friendships and the desire to better ones self through overcoming obstacles.  
Popular titles in this genre include: 

  • Dragon Ball
  • Hunter x Hunter 
  • ONE PIECE
  • Naruto 
Seinen

Seinen manga caters specifically to men's interest and is marketed to men between the ages 18 and 45. The content matter is usually a bit more mature which is what distinguishes it from shonen.   Where shonen manga tend to have straight-foward plots and characterization, manga that fall into this category tend to have more complex plots and protagonists who are morally grey. 
Popular titles in this genre include: 

  • Berserk 
  • Tokyo Ghoul 
  • Vagabond
  • Oyasumi Punpun
Shojo

The target demographic for this genre is young teenaged females.  Shojo literally means 'young woman' and has a variety of narrative styles.  Although shojo once primarily focused on romantic relationships in the 1990s the genre became more concerned with self-fulfillment with protagonists who often fought to protect their communities.  During this time the genre also began it's strong focus on emotional bonds between women.  
Popular titles in this genre include: 

  • Sailor Moon
  • Fruits Basket 
  • Orange
  • Namaikizakari 

*It is important to note that genre and demographic have heavy overlap and the most popular tropes used within these genres are often not a requirement.

THE BIG 3 

"The Big 3" is a term used in the west by the English speaking manga fandom and refers to the three best-selling titles that dominated sales in Japan in the early 2000s.  

Naruto

Arguably one of the most popular anime to come to the West in the early 2000s. From the chart below you can see that Naruto is the most consistent of The Big 3 with sales not dropping off until it ended its run in the 2014. Naruto also has a follow-up series titled Boruto.

One Piece

The undisputed best-selling manga of all time, ONE PIECE was the best-selling for 12 straight years until Demon Slayer took the title in 2019. ONE PIECE often ranks much higher in sales but is not the most popular of The Big 3 when speaking in an international context.

Bleach

 Bleach often ranks much lower than both Naruto and One Piece and the series place in The Big 3 is often debated due to the huge drop in popularity around the beginning of 2010 and the rise in popularity in titles such as Fairy Tail, Kuroko's Basketball and Silver Spoon.

TEN YEARS IN MANGA SALES

Fans have long wondered which Weekly Shonen Jump titles would become the new Big 3. A clear winner rose to the top in 2020 with Demon Slayer outselling the competition at almost eighty-three million volumes sold with the next bestseller, Kingdom, coming in at less than ten million copies.

Demon Slayer's success is unprecedented but also can be somewhat explained by anime and manga breaking into the general pop culture sphere around the end of the 2010s. Compared to the early 2000s manga and anime are both more readily available to consumers meaning Demon Slayer had a much easier time becoming a brand than titles that debuted in the early 2000s. Digital sales have also added to Demon Slayer's massive success while Naruto, Bleach and ONE PIECE all boasted huge physical sales during their respective runs.

ONE PIECE is an anomaly since it has been in print since 1999. There seems to be no end in sight and yet it has remained popular making Eiichiro Oda one of the best-selling writers of all time.
